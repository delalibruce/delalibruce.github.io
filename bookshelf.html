ranking is based off how distraught i was when the book ended

hot take: the end of a good book is a form of grief

*fyi i basically only read memoirs*

The Promise by Chaim Potok

sequel to the chosen. phenomenal book, potok is so skilled at grappeling with how one reconciles one's various conflicting identities. it's gripping and profound.

The Art of Gathering by Priya Parker

THIS BOOK. it's truly been my north star for every gathering and event i've hosted since. If you've ever wondered what makes a good gathering, party, wedding, funeral - this is for you.

The Chosen by Chaim Potok

if you couldn't tell already, i reallyyy love potok. the relationship between these two main characters and their respective identities. a must-read.

Shoe Dog by Phil Knight

funny story. wasn't into it at first, read the end, flipped to the beginning and finished the rest shortly after. phenomenal founders story. recommend!!

Between the World and Me by Ta-Nehisi Coates

beautiful, honest, really worth reading.

Biased by Jennifer Eberhardt

the topics in this book are so eye-opening. but what really makes it special is how incredible of a storyteller Eberhardt is, like truly it's on another level so much so that it's worth reading for that reason alone.

Talking to Strangers by Malcolm Gladwell

mr malcolm gladwell, the revisionist history legend himself. you cannot lie, my mans is an incredible journalist and storyteller. i am overdue to reread this book. certain anecdotes in the book felt irrelevant to topic at hand but that's just my opinion.

Small Fry by Lisa Brennan Jobs

crazy memoir. really interesting life experience and perspective on steve jobs as a person and father.

The Death of Ivan Ilyich by Leo Tolstoy

this book sold me on russian literature.

We Were Eight Years in Power by Ta-Nehisi Coates

collection of essays. between the world and me is a more personal, reflection and look into the black american experience.

Promised Land by Barack Obama

Haha read this book during my 75Hard, probably the only reason i finished it tbh. felt like most of the book was an elaborate justifaction for the controversial decisions he made while in office.

Minor Feelings by Cathy Park Hong

heavy on the memoir, less on educational perspective (re asian american identity) if that makes sense. felt very personal to hong.

Uncanny Valley by Anna Wierner

i've always been super fascinated by silicon valley culture. i think this book does an ok job at conveying this.

My Wife Said You May Want to Marry Me by Jason Rosenthal

2018 new york times article turned into a memoir with the same title. tragic story.

Crying in H Mart by Michelle Zauner

okay memoir-wise. felt a bit dragged on towards the end. lots to reflect on in terms of mother/daughter relationships.

Flash Boys by Michael Lewis

it's been awhile since i read this but i remember slugging through it.
